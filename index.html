Chapter 1: Understanding Prayer

As we begin into this journey, some of the first things we need to understand is prayer, for many new believers and even experienced Christians, prayer might feel intimidating it’s like you don’t want to mess up and you want to say the “right” words or even follow this specific formula to pray but in reality, prayer is simple prayer it’s just a simple honest conversation with God.

What is prayer? Prayer is the way we communicate with God just as God communicates to us through the Bible this is our way to communicate with him but it’s even more than this, it’s a way to draw closer to him to build a relationship with him, it’s a way for us to let go and just express our feelings our thoughts our worries our troubles our desires God invites us into prayer because he wants to have this relationship with us and like in any relationship communication is key.

Continuing my point, prayer is a way to communicate with him and not just as our creator, but as a loving father. As St. Augustine once said, “true prayer is nothing but love.”

Why is Prayer Important?

In the scriptures, we see it tells us in Thessalonians 5:17 “pray without ceasing” this of course doesn’t mean to go and pray 24/7 but that prayer should be a continuous, part of your life an ongoing conversation with God.

Prayer is very important because:

  • Builds our relationship with God. Like I stated before, in a very good relationship communication is always key.
  • Guides us in our daily lives. Through prayer we can seek wisdom, guidance, strength, protection when we must face challenges.
  • Helps us align our hearts with God’s will. Prayer isn’t just about asking God for things like I said before, its about seeking his guidance and trusting his plan for us.

How You Can Pray

Now that we’ve talked about “what is prayer” and “why is prayer important,” let’s take a look at some practical ways to pray. You don’t need fancy words or this specific formula, God is more interested in your heart more than your words because he knows what’s in your heart, he knows you more than anyone else would ever.

I’m going to list a few ways:

  • Be Honest with God: You don’t have to hide anything from God. Share your thoughts struggles and joys with him as you would with a close friend. You must remember, God already knows what on your heart, but he wants you to bring it to him.
  • Thank God for What You Have: Gratitude is a very powerful part of prayer. Start by thanking God for his blessing in your life, no matter how small they may be, thank him. This shifts your focus from things you don’t have to things you have been given.
  • Ask for Guidance: It’s okay to ask God for help and guidance we all need help sometimes. Whether it’s for big decisions or small daily choices God cares for you and about every aspect of your life.
  • Pray for Others: Prayer isn’t just about us. in the bible we are encouraged to pray for others the apostle James even tells us: “Therefore, confess your sins to one another and pray for one another, that you may be healed. The prayer of a righteous person has great power as it is working.” (James 5:16)

When you’re ready to pray again try appealing everything I said to your prayer, and even if you are not really sure let’s do a simple prayer,
Dear father, I come to you today with an open heart, Thank you for your love and your guidance in my life. Please help to grow closer to you and for me to understand your will I pray for those who read this book I hope that through this book they may also grow closer to you in Jesus’ name, amen.

Chapter 2: Understanding the Mother of God

As we continue in our journey, its very important to explore the role of Mary, the mother of Jesus Christ, very often called the Theotokos. This title, which means “God bearer” is crucial that you understand it to understand the incarnation which is how God became human. Now you might be asking, “Why is Mary even important” or “Why do we call her the Mother of God and what does it even mean?” lets dive right in this questions and understanding the Mother of God.

Who is Mary, the Theotokos?

Mary is not just any mother, she is the one who was chosen by God to Bear his Son, Jesus Christ. The title Theotokos emphasize that she gave birth not to just some human but someone who is fully God and fully human. The Belief of Mary as theotokos reminds us of the incredible love and grace of God because he didn’t just send anyone to save us, he sent his own son born of a virgin and entrusted her to Mary’s care.

Why Do We Honor Mary?

As Christians, we honor Mary because of her unique role in God’s plan. She was obedient to God’s will despite the challenges she faced. She teaches us about trust and surrender to God and reminds us that God can use us too no matter our circumstances. In the book of Luke we see Mary’s beautiful response to the angel Gabriel when she said, “I am the Lord’s servant. May your word to me be fulfilled.” (Luke 1:38) Mary shows us how we can respond to God’s call in our own lives.

What Can We Learn From Mary?

One of the many things we can learn from Mary is humility. Despite being chosen to bear the Son of God, she remained humble and open to God’s will. She faced criticism and skepticism but never wavered in her faith. We too can learn to embrace humility in our own lives and trust in God’s plan even when we don’t understand it.

Remember, honoring Mary doesn’t take away from our relationship with God but enhances it, as she points us back to her son Jesus Christ. She leads us closer to God and reminds us of his amazing grace.
